9. DO UNITED STATES RESIDENTS HAVE SPECIFIC PRIVACY RIGHTS?

If you reside in California, Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Florida, Indiana, Iowa, Kentucky, Maryland, Minnesota, Montana, Nebraska, New Hampshire, New Jersey, Oregon, Rhode Island, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, or Virginia, you may have rights to access, correct, obtain, or delete your personal information. You may also withdraw your consent to our processing. These rights may be limited by law. More details are provided below.

Will your information be shared with anyone else?

We share your personal information with service providers under contracts to support our services. We may also use your information internally for research and development, which isn't considered selling.

Your Rights

You have rights under certain US state data protection laws. However, these rights are not absolute, and in certain cases, we may decline your request as permitted by law. These rights include:

  • You have the right to know if we process your data, access it, correct errors, request deletion, and obtain copies of data you shared.
  • You cannot be discriminated against for exercising these rights.
  • You can opt out of targeted advertising or profiling that leads to significant legal effects.
  • Depending on your state, you may also have rights to:
    • Access categories of personal data processed.
    • Get lists of third parties we shared data with.
    • Review and correct how your data is profiled.
    • Limit use or disclosure of sensitive data.
    • Option out of data collection via voice or facial recognition.

How to Exercise Your Rights

You can exercise your rights by submitting a data subject access request, emailing official@safewox.com. In some US states, you may appoint an authorized agent to act for you, but we may deny requests if the agent doesn't provide valid proof of authorization as required by law.

Request Verification

When we receive your request, we'll verify your identity using the personal information you provide. If needed, we may ask for more details to confirm your identity and prevent fraud. If an authorized agent submits the request, they must provide written, signed permission from you.

Appeals

Under certain US state data protection laws, if we decline your request, you have the right to appeal by emailing us at official@safewox.com. We will respond in writing with the outcome and reasons for our decision. If your appeal is denied, you may file a complaint with your state attorney general.

California 'Shine The Light' Law

California Civil Code Section 1798.83, or the 'Shine The Light' law, lets California residents request, once a year for free, details about any personal information we shared with third parties for direct marketing in the past year, including the names and addresses of those third parties.
